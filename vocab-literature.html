Literature Vocabulary (30 Words)

Epiphany
Definition: A sudden and striking realization or insight, often used in literary contexts.
Synonyms: Revelation, awakening
Example: The character's epiphany at the end of the story changed her life forever.
Protagonist
Definition: The principal character in a literary work, who drives the plot forward.
Synonyms: Main character, central figure
Example: The protagonist’s ambition and flaws create the core conflict of the novel.
Stream of Consciousness
Definition: A narrative style that attempts to depict the multitudinous thoughts and feelings passing through the mind.
Synonyms: Interior monologue
Example: Stream of consciousness techniques allow readers deep access into the protagonist’s psyche.
Foreshadowing
Definition: A literary device that hints at upcoming events, building suspense for the reader.
Synonyms: Foretelling, hinting
Example: The darkening sky was a foreshadowing of the tragedy that would soon unfold.
Motif
Definition: A recurring element, symbol, or theme in a literary work.
Synonyms: Pattern, recurring idea
Example: The motif of mirrors emphasized the duality of the character’s identity.
Allusion
Definition: A reference, often indirect, to a person, place, event, or another literary work.
Synonyms: Indirect reference, hint
Example: The author’s allusion to Greek mythology enriched the narrative’s complexity.
Denouement
Definition: The final resolution or outcome of the plot after the climax.
Synonyms: Conclusion, resolution
Example: In the denouement, all the characters’ fates were decisively resolved.
Verisimilitude
Definition: The appearance of being true or real in a literary context.
Synonyms: Realism, authenticity
Example: Detailed descriptions gave the novel a sense of verisimilitude that immersed readers.
Euphemism
Definition: A mild or indirect word substituted for one considered too harsh or blunt.
Synonyms: Mild expression, understatement
Example: The writer used “passed on” as a euphemism for “died.”
Satire
Definition: A technique that uses humor, irony, or exaggeration to expose and criticize vices or follies.
Synonyms: Mockery, parody, lampoon
Example: The novel’s satire sharply criticized the government’s failings.
Ambiguity
Definition: An element in a text that can have multiple interpretations or meanings.
Synonyms: Uncertainty, vagueness
Example: The ambiguous ending left readers debating the character’s true motives.
Paradox
Definition: A statement that appears contradictory yet may reveal an underlying truth.
Synonyms: Contradiction, puzzle
Example: “Less is more” is a famous paradox relevant to literary style.
Anachronism
Definition: Something or someone misplaced in time, often a chronological inconsistency in literature.
Synonyms: Misplacement, chronological error
Example: The appearance of a digital watch in a medieval drama is an anachronism.
Caricature
Definition: An exaggerated portrayal of a character’s features or traits for comic or grotesque effect.
Synonyms: Exaggeration, distortion
Example: The political caricature lampooned the senator’s pompous speeches.
Archetype
Definition: A recurrent symbol or motif in literature, art, or mythology.
Synonyms: Prototype, classic example
Example: The hero’s journey is an archetype seen across various mythological traditions.
Metafiction
Definition: A form of literature that intentionally draws attention to the work’s status as a text.
Synonyms: Self-referential fiction
Example: The novel’s metafictional technique made characters aware they were in a story.
Polysyndeton
Definition: A rhetorical device involving the use of several conjunctions in close succession.
Synonyms: Over-conjunction
Example: Her sentence used polysyndeton to add rhythm: “I laughed and I cried and I cheered.”
Anaphora
Definition: The repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses or verses.
Synonyms: Repetitive start
Example: “We shall fight on the beaches, we shall fight on the landing grounds...” is a famous anaphora.
Denotation
Definition: The literal or primary meaning of a word, separate from its connotations.
Synonyms: Direct meaning
Example: While “home” has the denotation of a place where one lives, it also connotes comfort and family.
Connotation
Definition: The emotional or cultural associations attached to a word, beyond its literal meaning.
Synonyms: Implication, undertone
Example: The word “snake” often carries negative connotations of treachery or danger.
Bildungsroman
Definition: A literary genre focusing on the psychological and moral growth of the protagonist from youth to adulthood.
Synonyms: Coming-of-age story
Example: Many classic novels, like “Jane Eyre,” are considered bildungsromans.
Pathetic Fallacy
Definition: The attribution of human emotions or responses to nature, inanimate objects, or animals.
Synonyms: Emotional falsity, anthropomorphic emotion
Example: Using pathetic fallacy, the stormy weather reflected the character’s inner turmoil.
Hubris
Definition: Excessive pride or self-confidence, often leading to a character’s downfall in Greek tragedy.
Synonyms: Arrogance, overconfidence
Example: The king’s hubris blinded him to the possibility of defeat, ultimately causing his ruin.
Parable
Definition: A simple story used to illustrate a moral or spiritual lesson.
Synonyms: Fable, allegory
Example: Many religious texts use parables to convey ethical teachings.
Doppelgänger
Definition: A look-alike or double of a living person, often representing duality or hidden identity in literature.
Synonyms: Double, counterpart
Example: The character’s doppelgänger embodied his darker impulses.
Catharsis
Definition: The emotional release experienced by the audience at the end of a tragic drama.
Synonyms: Emotional purification
Example: Audience members often feel catharsis after witnessing the hero’s downfall.
Utopia
Definition: An imagined place or state of ideal perfection in laws, politics, and social conditions.
Synonyms: Paradise, perfect society
Example: Many novels describe utopias that eventually reveal hidden flaws.
Dystopia
Definition: An imagined state or society in which there is great suffering or injustice.
Synonyms: Oppressive society, anti-utopia
Example: Classic dystopias like “1984” warn about totalitarian control over citizens.
Trope
Definition: A common or overused theme or device in literary or artistic works.
Synonyms: Convention, cliché
Example: The “chosen one” trope often appears in fantasy literature.
