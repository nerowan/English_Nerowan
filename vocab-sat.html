SAT Vocabulary (Sample 10 Words)

Cacophony
Definition: A harsh, discordant mixture of sounds, often used metaphorically to describe chaos.
Synonyms: Dissonance, clamor
Example: The band’s rehearsal room produced a cacophony of out-of-tune instruments.
Grandiose
Definition: Impressive or magnificent in appearance or style, sometimes excessively so.
Synonyms: Majestic, pompous
Example: The billionaire’s grandiose mansion had thirty bedrooms and a private theater.
Obfuscate
Definition: To render obscure, unclear, or unintelligible; to confuse or bewilder.
Synonyms: Complicate, obscure
Example: The politician’s speech seemed designed to obfuscate rather than clarify his policies.
Apathetic
Definition: Showing or feeling no interest, enthusiasm, or concern.
Synonyms: Indifferent, unresponsive
Example: Voter turnout was low due to apathetic attitudes among the population.
Magnanimous
Definition: Very generous or forgiving, especially toward a rival or someone less powerful.
Synonyms: Noble, charitable
Example: His magnanimous gesture left a profound impression on the opposing team.
Quixotic
Definition: Extremely idealistic; unrealistic and impractical.
Synonyms: Visionary, romantic
Example: The plan to end world hunger in a single year was deemed quixotic but inspirational.
Pragmatic
Definition: Dealing with things sensibly and realistically, based on practical considerations.
Synonyms: Realistic, practical
Example: A pragmatic leader weighs both ideals and feasibility before making decisions.
Ruminate
Definition: To think deeply about something; to ponder or reflect.
Synonyms: Contemplate, mull over
Example: She needed time to ruminate on the scholarship offer before accepting it.
Esoteric
Definition: Intended for or likely to be understood by only a small number of people with a specialized knowledge.
Synonyms: Obscure, arcane
Example: The professor’s lectures were so esoteric that only a few students could keep up.
Hegemony
Definition: Leadership or dominance, especially by one country or social group over others.
Synonyms: Dominance, authority
Example: The empire’s cultural hegemony influenced neighboring regions for centuries.
