Archaeology Vocabulary (30 Words)

Excavate
Definition: To make a hole or channel by digging, often to uncover artifacts or structures.
Synonyms: Dig up, unearth
Example: Researchers plan to excavate the site to uncover ancient pottery.
Stratigraphy
Definition: The arrangement and study of rock layers (strata) or cultural layers in archaeology.
Synonyms: Layering, geological study
Example: Stratigraphy helps archaeologists date different layers of artifacts.
Artifact
Definition: An object made or used by humans, typically an item of cultural or historical interest.
Synonyms: Relic, antiquity, piece
Example: The bronze sword found at the dig site is a rare artifact.
Osteology
Definition: The study of the structure and function of bones, crucial for analyzing skeletal remains.
Synonyms: Bone study, skeletal analysis
Example: Osteology revealed that the population had high rates of arthritis.
Carbon Dating
Definition: A method for determining the age of organic materials based on their content of carbon-14.
Synonyms: Radiocarbon dating
Example: Carbon dating placed the burial site at approximately 2,000 years old.
In Situ
Definition: In its original place; used to describe artifacts found exactly where they were left.
Synonyms: Undisturbed, on-site
Example: Archaeologists prefer to analyze artifacts in situ before removing them.
Provenance
Definition: The place of origin or earliest known history of an artifact or archaeological find.
Synonyms: Origin, source, pedigree
Example: Determining the artifact’s provenance helps trace cultural exchanges.
Preservation
Definition: The protection and maintenance of artifacts or structures in their original state.
Synonyms: Conservation, protection
Example: Proper preservation techniques are required to keep the ancient scroll intact.
Tell
Definition: A mound formed over centuries by successive layers of human habitation and debris.
Synonyms: Archaeological mound
Example: The team excavated a tell that revealed multiple layers of human occupation.
Hypothesis
Definition: A proposed explanation for an observation, which can be tested through further investigation.
Synonyms: Theory, assumption
Example: Their hypothesis suggests trade networks extended much farther than previously believed.
Seriation
Definition: A relative dating method by ordering artifacts from multiple sites in chronological sequence.
Synonyms: Sequence dating
Example: Seriation helps compare pottery styles across different regions.
Midden
Definition: A deposit of refuse, shells, animal bones, and other waste found at archaeological sites.
Synonyms: Trash heap, dump
Example: Animal bones in the midden offered insights into the settlement’s diet.
Neolithic
Definition: Pertaining to the latter part of the Stone Age when ground or polished stone tools prevailed.
Synonyms: New Stone Age
Example: Neolithic pottery uncovered there displays intricate geometric designs.
Epigraphy
Definition: The study of inscriptions or epigraphs as writing; it is a primary tool of archaeology.
Synonyms: Inscription study
Example: Epigraphy revealed the name of a previously unknown ruler inscribed on a temple wall.
Conservation
Definition: The profession dedicated to preserving cultural property for the future.
Synonyms: Safeguarding, protection
Example: Conservation experts employed special chemicals to stabilize the ancient textiles.
Posthole
Definition: A hole dug in the ground to hold an upright post, often discovered as circular stains in the soil.
Synonyms: Post hole, structural pit
Example: A series of postholes suggested the location of a prehistoric wooden structure.
Sporadic
Definition: Occurring at irregular intervals or only in a few places; scattered or isolated.
Synonyms: Infrequent, random
Example: Sporadic evidence of trade goods indicated occasional contact with distant regions.
Exhumation
Definition: The process of removing a body or object from the ground after burial.
Synonyms: Digging up, disinterment
Example: Exhumation of the tomb uncovered jewelry and ceramics of significant historical value.
Chronometric Dating
Definition: Dating techniques that estimate the actual age of an object in years, such as carbon dating.
Synonyms: Absolute dating
Example: Chronometric dating placed the artifact within the 5th century BCE.
Typology
Definition: The systematic classification of artifacts according to their characteristics.
Synonyms: Classification, categorization
Example: By using typology, researchers identified the pottery as part of a rare style.
Material Culture
Definition: The physical objects, resources, and spaces that people use to define their culture.
Synonyms: Tangible heritage
Example: Studying material culture helps archaeologists understand daily life in ancient societies.
Shard
Definition: A piece of broken pottery or glass commonly found at archaeological sites.
Synonyms: Fragment, piece
Example: The shard’s decoration matched known Iron Age pottery styles.
Geophysical Survey
Definition: The use of non-invasive methods like ground-penetrating radar to detect subsurface anomalies.
Synonyms: Remote sensing
Example: A geophysical survey revealed possible foundations of an ancient building.
Assemblage
Definition: A group of artifacts found in close association, providing a snapshot of activity or culture.
Synonyms: Collection, grouping
Example: The pottery assemblage indicated a thriving trade in luxury ceramics.
Anthropometry
Definition: The measurement and analysis of the human body and its parts, often used in archaeology for skeletal studies.
Synonyms: Body measurements
Example: Anthropometry allowed the team to estimate the average height of the ancient population.
Microlith
Definition: A small, often flint or chert tool, characteristic of Mesolithic technology.
Synonyms: Tiny blade, small stone tool
Example: Microliths were commonly used as barbs for arrows in prehistoric cultures.
Ecofact
Definition: Natural remains such as seeds, bones, or pollen that provide environmental information about a site.
Synonyms: Natural remains, biofacts
Example: Ecofacts like charred seeds can reveal what crops were grown.
Paleoethnobotany
Definition: The study of ancient plant remains to understand past cultures’ diets and agriculture.
Synonyms: Archaeobotany
Example: Paleoethnobotany showed that millet was a staple in the region’s diet.
Relative Dating
Definition: Determining the age of artifacts or sites based on their relationship to other objects or layers.
Synonyms: Comparative dating
Example: By relative dating, the lower strata’s pottery was older than the surface finds.
Cultural Diffusion
Definition: The spread of cultural beliefs and activities from one group to another.
Synonyms: Cultural transmission, dissemination
Example: The presence of foreign motifs on local pottery hinted at cultural diffusion.
Archaeozoology
Definition: The study of animal remains from archaeological sites to understand human-animal interactions.
Synonyms: Zooarchaeology
Example: Archaeozoology revealed that domesticated goats were introduced earlier than believed.
