Gaokao Writing Practice

Overview

In the Chinese Gaokao English exam, writing tasks often feature “Li Hua,” a fictional student who needs to write letters or short essays. These compositions test your ability to communicate effectively in English, following a specific format and word limit.

Topics can include invitations, apologies, suggestions, or event announcements. Key points usually involve correct formatting (salutation, body, closing) and coherent paragraph structure.

General Writing Tips

  • Salutation & Opening: e.g., “Dear Tom,” or “Hi Mike,”. 1-2 sentences introducing who you are and why you’re writing.
  • Body / Details: Provide clear, concise points. For Li Hua style letters, mention time, place, reason, etc.
  • Closing & Signature: e.g. “Yours sincerely,” then “Li Hua.” Keep the tone polite and consistent.
  • Word Count: Usually around 80-120 words for Gaokao writing. Focus on clarity, not just length.
  • Common Expressions: “I’m writing to...” / “I would like to invite you to...” / “Thank you for your time...”

Sample Prompts (Li Hua)

Below are some common Li Hua scenarios to practice:

  • Prompt A: Li Hua wants to invite a foreign exchange student to the English Club event next Friday.
  • Prompt B: Li Hua needs to apologize to a foreign teacher for missing an appointment due to illness.
  • Prompt C: Li Hua writes to an international friend about the Spring Festival customs and invites them to join.
