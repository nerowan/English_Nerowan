TOEFL Vocabulary (Sample 10 Words)

Abundant
Definition: Existing or available in large quantities; plentiful.
Synonyms: Ample, copious
Example: The region is abundant in natural resources, ideal for economic development.
Consecutive
Definition: Following continuously; in unbroken or logical sequence.
Synonyms: Sequential, successive
Example: The city has experienced three consecutive years of population growth.
Hypothesize
Definition: To suggest a theory or idea that is not yet proven but that leads to further study or discussion.
Synonyms: Suppose, postulate
Example: Scientists hypothesize that the virus originated from a wildlife market.
Impartial
Definition: Treating all rivals or disputants equally; fair and just.
Synonyms: Unbiased, neutral
Example: An impartial judge is crucial for a fair trial.
Refute
Definition: To prove (a statement or theory) to be wrong or false; disprove.
Synonyms: Discredit, invalidate
Example: New evidence served to refute the old hypothesis.
Simulate
Definition: To imitate the appearance or character of; to produce a computer model of something.
Synonyms: Imitate, replicate
Example: Engineers used software to simulate the rocketâ€™s launch conditions.
Viable
Definition: Capable of working successfully; feasible.
Synonyms: Practical, achievable
Example: Solar energy has become a viable alternative to fossil fuels in many regions.
Advocate
Definition: To publicly recommend or support; a person who publicly supports a cause.
Synonyms: Promote, endorse
Example: The professor is a strong advocate for universal healthcare.
Attain
Definition: To succeed in achieving something that one has worked for.
Synonyms: Achieve, accomplish
Example: He worked diligently to attain fluency in English before the TOEFL test.
Integral
Definition: Necessary to make a whole complete; essential or fundamental.
Synonyms: Essential, indispensable
Example: Reading comprehension is integral to success on the TOEFL exam.
